Mind Body Spirit

The purpose of this work is to give the viewer an inside look into the artist's mind, body, and soul. Using images the audience is free to interpret the symbolism of the images as it relates to the artist. This triptych is separated into three collages, Mind, Body, and Spirit. The vision for the mind collage is to depict how my mind works and feels at times. I'm an overthinker so I found the image of â€œthe Thinker" to be fitting. There is a lot of other symbolism that lies within the collage, such as the night sky and the sponge. The sky representing my calm thinking space, and the sponge symbolic of my eagerness to learn and explore. The bridge between the spirit and mind is the body. The body collage is symbolic of my physical apperance as well as my heritage. The purple flower represents my hair, the mushroom my height, the flag my heritage, and the person is symbolic of how I feel most of the time. The last collage is the spirit collage. This collage represents who I am creatively and internally. These collages work together because although they are drastically different, the body collage helps to bridge the composition together and well as symbolically. The purpose of this work is exhibited when viewed left to right. Although the three are different they are bridged together by the body collage. Although each piece has its differences there are many similarities across the three that depict symbolic references to the artist.
